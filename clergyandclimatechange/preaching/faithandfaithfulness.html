John 11:32-44

Jesus said to them, “Unbind him, and let him go.”

those who build it labor in vain.

and go late to rest,

Notes: 1 Kings 17:8-16

The idea of people starving as the result of a famine is not an alien idea today, particularly as climate change continues to contribute to more extreme weather conditions in some of the most vulnerable countries of our world. But God’s compassion for the poor – even the poor who are not on our doorstep, who don’t share our nationality, is clear here.

Notes: Mark 13:1-8

Jesus talks surprisingly often in a way that seems to give creation ‘feelings’, even if in a metaphorical way. He talks about stones crying out, and here he describes the ‘birth pangs’ of the earth as they await the renewal of everything. I wonder how often we think about creation as having its own relationship to God, separate from our interaction with it? Certainly, the psalmist seems to talk that way!

Notes: 2 Samuel 23:1-7

Creation can give us pointers to how we can understand God. Here, living under God’s rule is like a beautiful, clear-skied, dewy morning.
